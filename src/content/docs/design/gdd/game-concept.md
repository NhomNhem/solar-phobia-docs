## Overview
A consequence-driven survival game set in a sun-scorched Vietnamese fishing village. By day, you choose who to save; by night, you run from what you left behind. Every choice has immediate, emotional gameplay impact.

## Player Fantasy
Experience the burden of leadership and the emotional weight of sacrifice. Feel the tension between calm, thoughtful days and chaotic, panicked nights.

## Core Loop
- **Day (Serve):** Choose which villagers to help (tea for light, incense for safe zones, offerings for skills). You can only save two out of three souls each day.
- **Choice:** One must be left behind—no “right” answer.
- **Night (Survive):** Flee to the shrine while pursued by the boss and haunted by the consequences of your daytime choice. The soul you left behind becomes a unique gameplay obstacle.

## Detailed Rules
- 2–3 NPCs, 1 boss, 1 map, 1 loop per vertical slice.
- Day actions: select resources, assign to NPCs, prepare for night.
- Night actions: navigate darkness, avoid hazards, use resources earned during the day.
- No complex minigames—focus is on impactful decisions.

## Tuning Knobs
- Number of NPCs (2–3)
- Boss difficulty
- Resource effectiveness (light, safe zones, skills)
- Map size and hazard density

## Acceptance Criteria
- One full day/night loop is playable
- Player choices have clear, immediate consequences
- Emotional tone matches concept
- All core systems (choice, consequence, survival) are implemented

## Anti-Pillars
- Not a management sim
- Not pure horror
- No grind or filler mechanics

## Art & Audio Direction
- Visual: Day—hot yellows; Night—deep blues; strong light/dark contrast
- Audio: Ocean waves, NPC whispers, escalating boss sounds

## Scope Lock
- 2–3 NPCs, 1 boss, 1 map, 1 loop
- Cut plan: remove 1 NPC, simplify boss, reduce endings if needed

## Player Takeaway
You don’t lose because the game is hard—you lose because of the choices you made.
